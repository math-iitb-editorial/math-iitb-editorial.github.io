The Emergence of the Editorial Team

Published: 2025-10-15

Welcome to the Mathematics Association Editorial Team! Now the natural follow-up question to the unusually cheery introduction from members of our wonderful department would be, "Who are we"? And frankly, there is no trivial response to such a question. There is the philosophical answer, which delves into the inner workings of the complex human mind, or there is the mathematical approach, where we proceed with structured detail on what we as an entity constitute. However, we will prove through contradiction that no simple explanation exists for this question. For if it did, what questions that are left to be unanswered would we have reason to overcommunicate to you about? Despite there being no clear answer to this problem, we will still attempt to provide some clarity.

We are a new unit within the Mathematics Association, responsible for bringing you the brand-new Mathematics Department Annual Newsletter! The contents of this newsletter remain a mystery to even the editors, but what we wish to impart is a sense of knowledge about the department's happenings, along with all that is beautiful and, perhaps, not so beautiful about the academic pursuit of mathematics. However, we are no strangers to the motivations of many in this department. We wish to be the voice of all those who set foot here, and with what diversity of opportunities (or potential lack thereof) that those who leave are met with. We wish to bring voices to them all. So join us on this journey as we discover what it means to be part of this department and all the colour it may bring to people’s lives.

Meet The Team

Department General Secretary: Ujjwal Kumar Mishra

We bring to you our Department General Secretary, Ujjwal Kumar Mishra. A man of big words and bullet point agendas, whenever in need of a conversation to break down the human psychology - he is the one to go too. Hyper-organised and pioneer of the department newsletter, he holds a strong belief in all voices getting a fair chance to be heard.

Chief Editor: Sahana Jahagirdar

We bring to you, Sahana Jahagirdar, Chief Editor of the Editorial Board. With her witty remarks and a little-too-honest sense of humour, she has promised to ensure her winding stories eventually get to the point. Her intensive background in creative writing and pursuit of artistic endeavours serve as a blatant side quest from her research pursuits. Her only aim in her tenure is to show that research "enthusiasts" can be "fun" too (the precise definition of enthusiasm and acts of enjoyment is left as an exercise to the reader).

Editor: Nilabha Saha

We bring to you, Nilabha Saha, an Editor of the Editorial Board, a self-proclaimed crusader against typographical sins, and a man whose idea of casual conversation includes debating font aesthetics, ranking song bridges by emotional damages, and deeply abstract philosophical dialogues. Known for treating LaTeX like a lifestyle choice and cryptography like a personality trait, he brings to the Board an alarming combination of precision, dramatic flair, and a habit of writing emails with more narrative structure than most novellas. While passionately rephrasing sentences for better dramatic cadence, he insists that good writing should be as satisfying as a clean compile and as elegant as a well-balanced TikZ diagram. Whether this is merely a snippet of an editorial article or a long-form performance art piece is, of course, left to interpretation.

Editor: Souhardya Biswas

Meet Souhardya Biswas, an Editor of the Editorial Board. A true sports enthusiast with a flair for words, Souhardya brings energy, curiosity, and a touch of humour to everything he writes. While sports remain his favourite playground, he’s always up for exploring new topics and perspectives. With his thoughtful take on stories and a sharp editorial eye, he’s all set to make this year’s editions both engaging and memorable.
